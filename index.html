Daniel Fisher - GMC - Pick Up

What was the hardest part of your truck to fix?

The hardest part was either the body work on the doors or else the frame.

How many vehicles have you rebuilt?

This was my first Copart/ salvage vehicle rebuild for my personal vehicle.

What was your budget on the Rebuild?

All in all I spent at least 10k not including time spent.

How long did it take from start to finish?

It took 4.5 months of working on it after work till late in the night and a lot of Saturdays too. I did the all the body work myself. This included stripping the paint the whole way to the factory primer, a bunch of dent repair work and then I primed and blocked the whole thing twice. A friend of mine did all the paint work. Then I sound dead-end the cab, installed aftermarket speakers and head-unit. Then reassembled the entire truck.

Why did you pick this truck?

I’ve always liked the older square body gm trucks. My first truck was a 98 Sierra, but it got demolished on snowy roads. The headache rack was the only thing I was able to salvage and put on my new truck.

Did you do it all yourself?

I did it mostly myself but got my friends to help with some things like the paint and tint work.

Tell us a little about yourself.

I work at an auto repair shop in Jonestown PA. After my fist truck was totaled, I couldn't afford a newer truck from a dealer so I bought an older one off Copart and rebuilt it. Since I did most of the work myself I was able to get a truck out of the south with no rust, restored with my own custom paint color, for a lot less money. Also I enjoy working with my hands.

Daniel Fisher

Thank you and it's my honor for you to feature my 68 International Harvester Scout 800.

What was the hardest part of your truck to fix?

The hardest part of the car to fix was changing all the rusted and thin metal with new 16–18-gauge steel. Specially all the floor pan.

How many vehicles have you rebuilt?

This is my first rebuild.

What was your budget on the Rebuild?

With the purchase of the car my budget was $16,000.

How long did it take from start to finish?

I started this project in March 2020 and I finished early August.

Why did you pick this truck?

I picked this truck because there are not many of these out there anymore, due to rust. I thought it would be great to revive and rebuilt a Scout 800 and give it more life.

Did you do it all yourself?

Mostly, I did use a friend's help for welding.

Tell us a little about yourself.

I am afterschool teacher in Burlington Vermont, also I own and run a food truck serving good Food to the community. I have always wanted to restore a car, and add some of my own design while doing a rebuilt. For example the laser engraving Teak wood dashboard I have designed and installed.

Herbert Hardaway - Honda CBR1000RR & Motorcycle

What was the hardest part of your truck to fix?

The hardest part of the motorcycle to fix was the front and rear brakes.

How many motorcycle have you rebuilt?

I have rebuilt two bikes including this one. The bikes are both Honda CBR1000RR! The first one was a 2006 the second one is a 2007. Same generation!!

What was your budget on the Rebuild?

I don’t think I initially had a budget. I was just trying to get it done and get it done correctly!! All new parts!! I’ve spent approximately $6,000 altogether! Including the bike! $3,500 for the bike. The remaining balance includes shipping the bike and motorcycle parts $2,500.

How long did it take from start to finish?

It took about 2 months from start to finish!! I had to wait for parts to come in that were ordered.

Why did you pick this truck?

I picked this motorcycle to rebuild because I’ve done it before! So the first bike I rebuilt was a 2006 Honda CBR1000RR and this is a 2007 Honda CBR1000RR. Same generation!! This made me very confident that I could do it again being that it was the same bike as the first!

Did you do it all yourself?

I absolutely did everything myself the first and the second time!

Tell us a little about yourself.

I am a US Army Veteran. What got me into rebuilding motorcycles was initially an accident. I got into an accident, fixed the bike and sold it! So I thought to myself I can do this again. This second time I saw a business opportunity I’ve envisioned in my head! I bought this bike to rebuild and resell! I like it so much I’ve thought about keeping it, but It’s probably going to get sold! I knew I could do it so I did it!!

Herbert Hardaway
