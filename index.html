Daniel Fisher - GMC - Pick Up

What was the hardest part of your truck to fix?

The hardest part was either the body work on the doors or else the frame.

How many vehicles have you rebuilt?

This was my first Copart/ salvage vehicle rebuild for my personal vehicle.

What was your budget on the Rebuild?

All in all I spent at least 10k not including time spent.

How long did it take from start to finish?

It took 4.5 months of working on it after work till late in the night and a lot of Saturdays too. I did the all the body work myself. This included stripping the paint the whole way to the factory primer, a bunch of dent repair work and then I primed and blocked the whole thing twice. A friend of mine did all the paint work. Then I sound dead-end the cab, installed aftermarket speakers and head-unit. Then reassembled the entire truck.

Why did you pick this truck?

I’ve always liked the older square body gm trucks. My first truck was a 98 Sierra, but it got demolished on snowy roads. The headache rack was the only thing I was able to salvage and put on my new truck.

Did you do it all yourself?

I did it mostly myself but got my friends to help with some things like the paint and tint work.

Tell us a little about yourself.

I work at an auto repair shop in Jonestown PA. After my fist truck was totaled, I couldn't afford a newer truck from a dealer so I bought an older one off Copart and rebuilt it. Since I did most of the work myself I was able to get a truck out of the south with no rust, restored with my own custom paint color, for a lot less money. Also I enjoy working with my hands.

Daniel Fisher

I don’t think I initially had a budget. I was just trying to get it done and get it done correctly!! All new parts!! I’ve spent approximately $6,000 altogether! Including the bike! $3,500 for the bike. The remaining balance includes shipping the bike and motorcycle parts $2,500.

Did you do it all yourself?
